    My 2025 Longevity Protocol | FULL DAY¶

    Overview¶

    Bryan Johnson presents his comprehensive daily longevity protocol, documenting the specific practices and interventions he implements to optimize health and lifespan. Based on three years of work with a 30-person medical team measuring biological age across all organs, Johnson claims to have achieved the best biomarkers and healthspan metrics of anyone in the world. This full-day breakdown covers sleep optimization, morning routines, exercise, nutrition, targeted therapies, and evening wind-down strategies.

    Key Insights¶

    • Sleep is the #1 life priority: Johnson sleeps by 8:30 PM and wakes naturally around 5:00 AM, achieving over 2 hours of REM sleep, 2 hours of deep sleep, and less than 30 minutes of wakefulness per night
    • Resting heart rate is the strongest predictor of sleep quality; all daily practices are designed to lower beats per minute
    • Morning light exposure: 10,000 lux light therapy for 3-4 minutes within minutes of waking to regulate circadian rhythm
    • Daily caloric intake: 2,250 calories composed of 25% protein, 35% carbs, and 40% fat on a plant-based diet (excluding collagen peptides)
    • Exercise: 60-minute morning workout including activities like pickleball, biking, climbing, plus 5-minute movement breaks every 30 minutes throughout the workday
    • Advanced therapies: Red and near-infrared light therapy (6 minutes daily), hyperbaric oxygen therapy, and focused shockwave therapy applied to entire body
    • Body composition tracking: Daily measurements using smart scale to monitor fat, muscle, hydration, weight, and arterial stiffness
    • Body temperature optimization: Achieved a 5°F drop in core temperature through metabolic efficiencies
    • Liquid intake stopped by 4 PM to prevent nighttime bathroom disruption
    • Achieved 8 months of perfect sleep scores in human history

    Core Concepts¶

    Sleep as Foundation: Sleep is positioned as the primary lever for longevity, with quality metrics (REM/deep sleep duration, minimal awakenings) directly correlated to all other health outcomes.

    Circadian Rhythm Optimization: Anchoring sleep and wake times, morning light exposure, and controlled sun exposure throughout the day to synchronize biological clocks.

    Daily Macro-Pulsing: Structured meal timing (breakfast post-workout, lunch mid-workday, final meal before afternoon work) to optimize nutrient utilization and energy levels.

    Movement Integration: Breaking up sedentary work with 5-minute activity bursts every 30 minutes to maintain blood flow, mental flexibility, and physical range of motion.

    Multi-Modal Therapy Stacking: Combining light therapy, hyperbaric oxygen, shockwave therapy, and other interventions to address biological aging across multiple pathways simultaneously.

    Resting Heart Rate as Biomarker: Using RHR as the primary health metric that influences sleep quality and serves as a window into overall cardiovascular and autonomic nervous system function.

    Biomarker-Driven Personalization: Measuring biological age of individual organs using a team of 30 medical professionals to guide protocol adjustments.

    Practical Takeaways¶

    1. Establish a consistent sleep schedule: Aim for 8:30 PM bedtime with natural wake time around 5:00 AM; prioritize sleep as your #1 health lever.

    2. Implement morning light therapy: Get 10,000 lux of light exposure within 3-4 minutes of waking to anchor your circadian rhythm (consider a light therapy box if sunlight isn't available).

    3. Track resting heart rate daily: Use this as your primary health metric; all other interventions should be evaluated on their ability to lower RHR.

    4. Structure your day around peak cognitive hours: Schedule your most important and creative work in the first few hours after waking when alertness is highest.

    5. Add movement breaks every 30 minutes: Incorporate 5 minutes of light activity (walking, stretching, bodyweight exercises) during work to maintain circulation and mental clarity.

    6. Adopt a plant-based whole-food diet: Focus on vegetables, nuts, seeds, berries, legumes, and quality fats (olive oil); track macronutrient ratios (25% protein, 35% carbs, 40% fat).

    7. Create an evening wind-down protocol: Start 2-3 hours before bed with screen cessation, light reading, meditation, or breath work to lower resting heart rate before sleep.

    Key Learnings & Conclusions¶

    Bryan Johnson's 2025 protocol demonstrates that comprehensive health optimization requires systems-level thinking where sleep quality serves as the foundational metric that influences all downstream health outcomes. The protocol emphasizes that biological age can be measured, monitored, and reversed through multimodal interventions (light therapy, movement, nutrition, advanced therapies) that work synergistically rather than in isolation. The consistency of daily practice—from precise sleep timing to integrated movement breaks—appears as important as any individual intervention, suggesting that longevity is achieved through disciplined protocol adherence rather than occasional intensive efforts. This data-driven, measurable approach to health offers a replicable framework for others seeking to optimize healthspan and lifespan.
