2. Telomere extension: Telomeres are the protective caps at the ends of chromosomes that shorten with each cell division. When telomeres become critically short, cells can no longer divide, leading to aging and eventual death. Research into telomere extension aims to prevent or reverse this process, potentially slowing down by halting aging.

3. Senolytic: These are drugs or chemicals that selectively remove senescent cells—cells that have stopped dividing and contribute to aging-related diseases. Eliminating senescent cells may reduce inflammation, improve tissue function, and potentially extend healthy lifespans.

4. Genetic modification: Advances in genetic engineering, such as CRISPR-Cas9 technology, could potentially be used to modify genes associated with aging or age-related diseases, potentially extending lifespans and improving overall health.
