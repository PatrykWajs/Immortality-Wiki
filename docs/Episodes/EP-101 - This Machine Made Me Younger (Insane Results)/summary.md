Bryan Johnson undertakes a 60-session hyperbaric oxygen therapy (HBOT) protocol over 90 days to test whether this accessible longevity therapy delivers measurable results. ## Core Concepts

**Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (HBOT)**
Pressurizing a chamber to 2 ATA (equivalent to 33 feet below water) while breathing 100% oxygen allows the body to be saturated with pure oxygen, enhancing mitochondrial function, accelerating healing, and potentially slowing aging.

**Telomeres**
Protective caps at the ends of chromosomes that shorten with age, functioning as a "biological clock." Studies show HBOT can increase telomere length by up to 38% in older populations, with gains equivalent to reversing years of cellular aging.

**Senescent Cells**
Aging cells that emit pro-inflammatory cytokines ("bad things" in the body). HBOT reduced senescent cell burden by up to 37% in studies, contributing to reduced inflammaging.

**Inflammaging**
Chronic systemic inflammation linked to age-related disease and dysfunction. HBOT appears uniquely effective at reducing inflammation to undetectable levels across the entire body.

**Dose Response in Longevity**
The principle that optimal benefit requires precise dosing—too little yields no effect, too much may be counterproductive. Proposed maintenance: 20 sessions every 3 months after initial 60-session protocol.

## Practical Takeaways

1. **Find HBOT access locally**: Install a chamber at home or identify a nearby clinic to enable daily 90-minute sessions; logistical friction is the primary barrier to completion
2. **Prioritize baseline and post-protocol biomarker testing**: Measure inflammation, VEGF, gut health markers, cognitive biomarkers (PAL127), telomere length, and skin health via multispectral imaging before and after therapy
3. **Build HBOT into your daily workflow**: Use the 90-minute sessions for focused work on high-priority projects to increase adherence and make the protocol sustainable long-term
4. **Follow a constrained protocol timeline**: Sessions must be completed within a specific timeframe (60 sessions in 90 days) rather than spread out; consistency matters for biomarker improvements
5. **Consult your physician before starting**: HBOT may interact with certain medical conditions; individual protocols should be tailored with medical oversight
6. **Expect psychological challenges**: Prepare for claustrophobia, mask discomfort, and mental monotony; having permitted electronics (since the chamber interior is 21% oxygen) transforms the experience
7. **Budget for accessibility improvements**: As HBOT becomes more popular and systems become cheaper, wider access will increase; advocate for evidence-based certification standards at clinics

## Key Learnings & Conclusions

Hyperbaric oxygen therapy represents a genuinely efficacious, accessible longevity intervention with measurable whole-body benefits including complete inflammation elimination, dramatic vascular growth signaling, gut barrier improvement, cognitive biomarker gains, and telomere elongation. The key to success is consistent daily execution within a constrained timeframe and comprehensive biomarker measurement pre and post-protocol. Bryan Johnson's results suggest HBOT should be prioritized among available longevity therapies, though optimal maintenance dosing remains an open question requiring further experimentation.
