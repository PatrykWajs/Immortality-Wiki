## Overview

Bryan Johnson travels to Prospera, an experimental governance zone off the coast of Honduras, to receive myostatin inhibition gene therapy from Minicircle—a cutting-edge biohacking company. The procedure uses plasmid-based gene therapy with a built-in kill switch to potentially extend human lifespan, break the 120-year biological ceiling, and improve muscle mass and epigenetic age. This represents one of the most extreme anti-aging interventions Johnson has undertaken as part of his Blueprint longevity protocol.

## Key Insights

- **Myostatin inhibition gene therapy** ranks seventh among the best-performing lifespan extenders in Johnson's research, demonstrating mice living 30% longer after receiving it
- **Cost is approximately $220,000** for the procedure, making it accessible only to high-net-worth individuals currently
- The therapy uses a **circular plasmid DNA structure** that is subcutaneously injected and can be loaded with any desired gene
- **Built-in kill switch mechanism**: tetracycline antibiotic can instantly deactivate the injected DNA if adverse reactions occur
- Animal models show **significant muscle mass increases, improved epigenetic age, and telomere lengthening** in recipients
- The procedure is **not FDA approved in the United States**, requiring travel to Prospera's regulatory-free zone
- Gene therapy targets the myostatin pathway, similar to natural mutations seen in Belgian Blue and bully whippet dog breeds

## Core Concepts

### Myostatin Inhibition
A gene therapy approach that blocks myostatin, a protein that limits muscle growth. This naturally occurring mutation appears in certain dog breeds and cattle, resulting in dramatically increased muscle mass and strength.

### Minicircle Technology
A plasmid-based delivery system using circular DNA loops that are easy to manufacture, subcutaneously inject, and can be customized with different genes for various therapeutic benefits.

### Built-in Kill Switch
A safety mechanism using tetracycline antibiotic that can rapidly deactivate injected DNA molecules if the body has an adverse reaction, theoretically making the therapy reversible.

### The 120-Year Ceiling
The maximum human lifespan observed to date, which gene therapy and other advanced interventions aim to extend by addressing fundamental aging mechanisms.

### Prospera Zone
An experimental governance jurisdiction off Honduras that operates under different regulatory frameworks, enabling access to cutting-edge medical procedures not yet approved by traditional regulatory bodies like the FDA.
