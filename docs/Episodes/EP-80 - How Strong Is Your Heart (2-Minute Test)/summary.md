## Overview

Heart rate recovery (HRR) — the drop in beats per minute within one minute of stopping exercise — is a critical biomarker for cardiovascular health and longevity. This video explains how to measure HRR at home, why it matters, and provides evidence-based strategies to improve it through exercise, diet, and sleep.

## Key Insights

- Heart rate recovery is measured by exercising vigorously, stopping, and measuring the drop in heart rate after exactly one minute
- Bryan Johnson's personal HRR is 37 beats per minute, placing him in the 75th percentile for elite athletes and 99th percentile for the general population
- Low HRR is associated with increased risk of coronary artery disease, heart failure, hypertension, metabolic syndrome, diabetes, and sleep apnea
- A New England Journal of Medicine study of over 2,000 participants found those with low HRR had 400% higher all-cause mortality risk over six years
- HRR is a predictive marker of future health outcomes and mortality risk
- Three primary interventions improve HRR: structured exercise (150 min/week moderate + 75 min/week vigorous), optimized diet (high fiber, adequate protein, avoid junk food/smoking/vaping), and quality sleep (7-8 hours)

## Core Concepts

**Heart Rate Recovery (HRR):** The number of beats per minute your heart rate drops within 60 seconds after stopping vigorous exercise. A higher HRR indicates better cardiovascular fitness and autonomic nervous system recovery.

**Cardiovascular Biomarker:** HRR serves as a predictive indicator of future disease risk and mortality, making it a valuable tool for assessing overall health trajectory and intervention effectiveness.

**Exercise Intensity Classification:** Moderate exercise allows conversation during activity; vigorous exercise is intense enough that speech becomes impossible.

## Practical Takeaways

1. Measure your heart rate recovery at home using a heart rate monitoring device — exercise vigorously, note your peak heart rate, rest for exactly 60 seconds, and measure the drop
2. Aim for at least 150 minutes per week of moderate-intensity exercise and 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise to improve HRR
3. Optimize your diet by increasing dietary fiber and protein intake while eliminating smoking, vaping, and processed junk food
4. Prioritize 7-8 hours of quality sleep per night, focusing on minimizing time to fall asleep and time awake during the night
5. Use your HRR as a trackable biomarker to monitor the effectiveness of lifestyle interventions over time
6. Compare your HRR results against age-appropriate percentile charts (elite athlete vs. general population) to contextualize your cardiovascular fitness level

## Key Learnings & Conclusions

Heart rate recovery is a powerful, easily-measurable biomarker that predicts cardiovascular health and longevity risk with significant predictive power — low HRR correlates with 400% higher mortality risk. The good news is that HRR responds well to three modifiable lifestyle factors: structured exercise, dietary optimization, and sleep quality. This makes HRR an ideal metric for individuals committed to longevity optimization, as improvements can be tracked regularly and are directly linked to reduced disease risk and extended healthspan.
