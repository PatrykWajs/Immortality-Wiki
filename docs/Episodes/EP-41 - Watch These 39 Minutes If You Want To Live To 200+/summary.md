## Core Concepts

**Blueprint Mindset ("Don't Die")**
A future-focused framework where individuals adopt the algorithmic perspective that the primary goal is not dying. Like the germ theory example from 1870, this mindset embraces uncomfortable truths about longevity and rejects the default narrative that death is inevitable. It positions health optimization as humanity's one unifying objective that transcends tribalism.

**Five Power Laws of Health**
The foundational interventions with the strongest evidence base for extending lifespan and healthspan: no smoking, strategic exercise, optimal BMI, Blueprint diet, and minimal alcohol. These deliver baseline life extension of ~32 years and form the prerequisite foundation before pursuing advanced therapies.

**Evening Brian (Behavioral Segmentation)**
Johnson's psychological technique for managing difficult impulses by naming and compartmentalizing the part of himself that sabotages health (evening Brian appeared at 7 PM seeking comfort foods). By treating this as a separate entity with documented arguments, Johnson gained control through dialogue rather than willpower alone, eventually eliminating all negative behaviors.

**Democratic Self vs. Monarchical Self**
A reframing of self-governance: instead of the "monarch mind" making arbitrary decisions (eating brownies because it wants them), shift to a "democratic system" that queries the body's actual needs through biomarkers and scientific evidence, then creates protocols to satisfy organ-specific requirements. The body becomes self-managing while the mind observes.

**Organ-Specific Aging**
Understanding that chronological age is misleading—instead measure biological age of each organ system separately (e.g., "my heart is functionally age 35, my ears are age 64 due to shooting range exposure"). This allows targeted interventions on the most aged systems and reveals hidden vulnerabilities.

**The Power Law Ranking System**
Blueprint evaluated every published longevity intervention, ranked by effect size on lifespan/healthspan, and scored believability of evidence. This created a prioritized list of ~500 interventions, allowing focus on highest-ROI treatments rather than infinite personalization details.

- **Prioritize sleep above all else**: Make it non-negotiable; track resting heart rate as a sleep quality predictor; aim for 46 BPM or lower as Johnson demonstrates; use temperature-controlled mattress and blackout environment if possible
