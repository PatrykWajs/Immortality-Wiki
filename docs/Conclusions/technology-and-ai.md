### [EP-130 — I Did Mushrooms for Science, Pt. 1](../Episodes/EP-130 - I Did Mushrooms for Science, Pt. 1/summary.md)

Psychedelics represent a potentially significant but underexplored longevity tool—when properly measured and contextualized, they may trigger rejuvenation mechanisms at the cellular and systemic level. Bryan Johnson's approach of applying rigorous biomarker tracking and brain imaging to the psychedelic experience creates a new scientific paradigm that could reframe how society understands these compounds beyond recreation or psychiatric treatment. The integration of advanced measurement technology (Kernel Flow) with comprehensive blood work demonstrates that subjective experiences can now be paired with objective biological data, enabling evidence-based longevity research on previously unmeasured territories. This experiment suggests that understanding the neurobiological and physiological cascades triggered by psychedelics may unlock novel pathways for cellular repair and human optimization.

As this technology scales and algorithms improve with more data, brain age may become a primary biomarker for assessing the effectiveness of anti-aging and cognitive preservation interventions.

### [EP-94 — Creatine, Sleep Trackers, and AI Doctors | Bryan Johnson Podcast](../Episodes/EP-94 - Creatine, Sleep Trackers, and AI Doctors - Bryan Johnson Podcast/summary.md)

More importantly, creatine exemplifies how supplements often show benefits across multiple biological domains (cognition, bone, performance) even when one metric (muscle mass) shows null results in limited populations. The deeper theme across all three topics—creatine, AI doctors, and sleep tracking—is that measurement + awareness creates wisdom: whether measuring creatine saturation, your sleep-cognition link, or your body's compensation mechanisms, the pairing of objective data with subjective experience is the foundation for effective health optimization and realistic assessment of aging speed.
